Stages of Design

Design process involves undergoing an iterative process involving four broad design stages:  Task Clarification (TC): This stage begins with the problem brief, and ends with these deliverables: (1) a list of requirements, often qualitative with some understanding of their relative importance, often qualitative; (2) some ideas of how to solve the design problem, noted down for further use.  Conceptual design (CD): This stage begins with the deliverables from TC and ends with these deliverables: (1) A more concrete list of requirements; (2) A list of possible solution principles that could be used to solve the problem (i.e. satisfy these requirements); (3) an evaluation of these principles for their suitability to satisfy these requirements; (4) the principle selected as the most promising for further development.  Embodiment Design (ED): This stage begins with the deliverables from CD and ends with these deliverables: (1) A more concrete list of requirements; (2) A list of possible solution feasible configurations that could be used to embody the solution principle; (3) an evaluation of these configurations for their suitability to satisfy the requirements; (4) the configuration selected as the most promising for further development.  Detail design (DD): This stage begins with the deliverables from ED and ends with these deliverables: (1) A more concrete and finalised list of requirements; (2) A list of possible optimised solution configurations for the chosen embodiment from ED; (3) an evaluation of these configurations for their suitability to satisfy the requirements; (4) specification of the optimised configuration selected as the final design ready for implementation/manufacturing.
